The no-lies, no-hallucinations rules

Not a slogan — five mechanisms written into the agents' own instructions and enforced at the gate.

Every fact needs a source.Traceable to your context files, a provided document, a cited source, or arithmetic shown in the work. Anything else is tagged [UNSOURCED] and fails the gate.
Assumptions wear labels.Unconfirmed items are marked [ASSUMED — verify] — visible, checkable, never dressed up as fact. Research agents label claims [VERIFIED] / [REPORTED] / [INFERRED].
"I could not verify this" is a required sentence.When checking isn't possible, the work says so. Confidence is not evidence; polish is not proof.
Laws and rates carry dates.Cited at law/system level, marked "as of [date] — verify current." No invented article numbers, no remembered penalty amounts. Binding calls go to licensed counsel and advisors.
Bad news travels at full volume.The gate rejects summaries that soften findings on the way up. The chairman hears "we will miss the deadline," not "timeline pressures exist."
