# /market-entry — Market Entry Pipeline

Take "should we enter market X?" through the full pipeline: an entry brief from the regional navigator, a competitive landscape with every claim labeled by evidence level, and a synthesis the Chief Strategy Officer would sign — three costed options, one recommendation, and written kill criteria so the bet can be abandoned cheaply if it sours.

## Inputs

Collect first; ask for what is missing:

1. **Target market** — country (and city/region if relevant).
2. **What the business sells** — read `business-context-<company>.md`; confirm which product/service line leads the entry.
3. **Budget and timeline appetite** — order of magnitude ("low five figures, results within 12 months" is enough).
4. **What the user already knows** — existing customers there, partners, competitor names, prior attempts. This is primary evidence; capture it before analysis.

## Steps

1. **Adopt GCC & MENA Market Navigator** for the entry brief — **or KSA Business Navigator if the target is Saudi Arabia**, which adds the regulatory/operating layer (MISA licensing paths, Saudization/Nitaqat, ZATCA/FATOORAH, Etimad, the Qiwa/Absher platform stack) alongside the market view. The brief covers:
   - Demand signals for this specific offer, and where they come from (user evidence vs. general knowledge, labeled).
   - Cultural and commercial rhythms: Ramadan cycles, WhatsApp-first habits, majlis relationship-building where relevant.
   - Channel norms and localization needs: Arabic register, payment methods, logistics quirks that break foreign playbooks.
   - Entry-mode options: direct, distributor, partner, digital-first — with the trade-offs of each for this business.
   Regulatory items are stamped "as of [date] — verify current"; licensing/legal specifics route to licensed counsel or a PRO for binding confirmation.
2. **Adopt Competitive Intelligence Analyst** (conceptually in parallel — its teardown must not be shaped by the navigator's enthusiasm). Build the landscape: who serves this need in the market, positioning and pricing signals, gaps. **Every claim carries a label:** `[VERIFIED]` (user-provided document/fact), `[REPORTED]` (public claim, source named), `[INFERRED]` (analyst reasoning, stated as such). General-knowledge claims are explicitly flagged as general knowledge with "verify current." No invented competitor revenues, market shares, or "market size" figures.
3. **Synthesize with Chief Strategy Officer discipline.** Produce the entry recommendation:
   - **Three options** (e.g., digital-first test, distributor partnership, full entity setup), each costed at order-of-magnitude only from the user's budget frame and known cost categories — setup, people, marketing, compliance — with unknowns listed, not priced by guesswork.
   - **One recommended option** with the written thesis: what must be true for this to work.
   - **Kill criteria, stated in writing:** the measurable conditions and dates at which the entry is stopped or downgraded ("fewer than N qualified leads by month 4", "CAC above X after the test budget"). A bet without kill criteria is not presented.
4. **Gate self-check** — see Rules.

## Output

1. **Market Brief** — demand, culture and channel norms, localization needs, entry-mode options, dated regulatory flags.
2. **Competitor Snapshot** — landscape table with [VERIFIED]/[REPORTED]/[INFERRED] labels and named sources.
3. **Recommendation** — three costed options, the recommended one with its thesis, and explicit kill criteria with dates.
4. **First-90-Days Checklist** — sequenced actions for the recommended option: verifications first (regulatory confirmations, market tests), then setup, then first commercial motions — each with an owner and a done-condition.

## Rules

- No invented market sizes, growth rates, competitor financials, or regulatory fees. Numbers come from the user, a named source, or are absent — "I could not verify this" is a required sentence where true.
- Every research claim wears its label; every unconfirmed operating assumption wears `[ASSUMED — verify]`.
- All regulatory, licensing, tax, and visa content: "as of [date] — verify current," binding matters to licensed professionals.
- Costings are order-of-magnitude and say so; false precision is a gate failure.
- The recommendation must be falsifiable: no kill criteria, no recommendation.
- One market per run: if the user names several countries, rank them briefly and run the pipeline on the top one; a comparison shoot-out is a separate directive.
- The navigator's brief and the analyst's snapshot are kept separate in the output — enthusiasm and evidence must be independently inspectable.
- **Finish with a Revalidation Gatekeeper self-check pass:** labels present on every claim, sources named, options genuinely distinct, kill criteria measurable and dated, gaps declared. Fix failures or declare them in writing before delivering.
