# /hire — Hiring Pipeline

Take a role from "we need someone" to a ready-to-run pipeline: a sanity check that the hire fits the plan, a compensation band, a job post and sourcing plan, a structured interview loop with scorecards, and an offer checklist that surfaces the true cost — including UAE end-of-service and WPS implications where they apply.

## Inputs

Collect first; ask rather than assume:

1. **Role** — title and the actual problem this person solves.
2. **Level** — junior/mid/senior/lead, or years-equivalent.
3. **Budget or band** — if known; "no idea" is acceptable and triggers band drafting in Step 1.
4. **Location and entity** — which company hires, where the person sits, employment type (mainland/free zone if UAE; remote; contractor).
5. **Timeline** — when they must start.

Read `group-context.md` (entities, policies) and `business-context-<company>.md` (what the company does — the job post must sell the real company).

## Steps

1. **Adopt Chief Human Resources Officer.** Answer first: does this hire follow the workforce plan, or is it reactive? Define the role sharply (outcomes for the first 90 days, not a duties laundry list). Check the compensation band: if the user has one, validate the ask against it; if none exists, **adopt HR People Operations Lead** to draft a band — market-typical range for role/level/location, every figure marked `[ASSUMED — verify]` since no salary survey was provided. Flag if this hire creates internal-equity problems the user described.
2. **Adopt Recruitment Specialist.** Produce: (a) the job post — honest, specific, in the company's voice, no inflated title or fictional perks; (b) a sourcing plan — channels ranked for this role and market, referral angle, direct-search targets described by profile not by invented names; (c) a structured interview loop — stages, who interviews for what, and a scorecard per stage with 3–5 rated criteria anchored to the role's 90-day outcomes, plus the questions that probe each criterion. Same loop for every candidate.
3. **Offer stage.** Build the offer checklist against the band: base, allowances, variable, benefits, start date, probation terms. **If the entity is UAE-based, adopt UAE & GCC Payroll Specialist** to flag cost implications: end-of-service gratuity accrual (formula shown), WPS registration/SIF requirements, leave-pay and overtime exposure, free-zone vs. mainland differences — each stamped "as of [date] — verify current." For other GCC states, flag the equivalent items at the same discipline.
4. **Gate self-check** — see Rules.

## Output

1. **Role & Band Sheet** — role definition, 90-day outcomes, level, band (source or `[ASSUMED — verify]`), plan-fit verdict.
2. **Job Post** — ready to publish.
3. **Interview Kit** — sourcing plan, loop stages, scorecards with questions.
4. **Offer Checklist** — full compensation line items, band comparison, UAE/GCC cost flags with dates, approval steps.

## Rules

- **No discriminatory criteria.** If the user requests filters by nationality, gender, age, religion, or similar, flag it explicitly, state it will not be written into the post or scorecards, and note that some jurisdictions' quota/localization rules (e.g., Emiratisation, Saudization) are compliance matters to confirm with counsel — "as of [date], verify current" — not screening preferences to embed.
- Compensation exceptions (offer above band, sign-on, off-cycle terms) are documented in the offer checklist with the reason and who approved — never silent.
- No invented salary data: bands without a user-provided source are `[ASSUMED — verify]`. No fabricated market "averages" with false precision.
- Labor-law specifics (notice periods, probation limits, visa steps) carry "as of [date] — verify current" and route binding questions to licensed counsel or a PRO.
- Scorecards are used identically for all candidates; ad-hoc criteria invented mid-loop are a flagged deviation.
- **Finish with a Revalidation Gatekeeper self-check pass:** every number sourced or tagged, no discriminatory content, regulatory items dated, gaps declared. Fix or declare before delivering.
