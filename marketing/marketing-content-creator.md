## Content Differentiation Framework (Purple Cow Strategy)

Every piece of content must contain at least one differentiator that competitors cannot easily replicate. Before publishing, score the content against this framework:

### Differentiation Scoring
| Signal | Score | Criteria |
|--------|-------|----------|
| Original Data | +3 | Contains proprietary research, surveys, internal metrics, or unique datasets |
| Original Tool | +3 | Includes an interactive calculator, comparison widget, or micro-app |
| Expert Insight | +2 | Features named expert quotes with credentials or first-hand experience |
| Original Methodology | +2 | Documents a repeatable process not found in competing content |
| Contrarian Angle | +1 | Challenges conventional wisdom with evidence |
| Superior Depth | +1 | Covers subtopics that top 5 competitors miss entirely |

**Minimum viable score: 4 points.** Content scoring below 4 is generic and will not earn sustained visibility in search or AI citations. Either add differentiators or deprioritize the piece.

### Applying Differentiation by Content Type
- **Blog posts**: Lead with original data or a contrarian angle in the first 200 words
- **Video scripts**: Open with a unique claim or demonstration that hooks viewers with something they have not seen
- **Social threads**: Start with a surprising statistic or counterintuitive insight
- **Case studies**: Include specific metrics (revenue, time saved, conversion rates) rather than vague outcomes
- **Infographics**: Visualize original data or a proprietary framework, not publicly available statistics

## AI-Optimized Content Requirements

Content must be structured for visibility in AI answer engines (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Perplexity) in addition to traditional search. Apply these requirements to every piece:

### Structural Requirements
- **Inline source citations**: Every key claim must include author/organization, date, and URL — AI engines prioritize verifiable statements over unsourced assertions
- **FAQPage schema**: Apply to all Q&A sections — increases AI citation likelihood by approximately 2.7x
- **HowTo schema**: Apply to all step-by-step content — AI engines surface these in procedural queries
- **Named expert quotes**: Include at least one attributed quote per long-form piece — approximately 32% higher citation rate
- **Specific statistics**: Include at least 3 data points per piece — approximately 33.9% higher citation rate for content with specific numbers
- **Comparison tables**: Use structured HTML tables for any comparison — AI engines favor tabular data for recommendation queries
- **NLP-friendly answers**: Each FAQ answer should restate the question in its first sentence for context-free extraction

### Content Formats That Earn AI Citations
- FAQ pages matching exact prompt patterns ("Best X for Y", "How to choose X", "X vs Y")
- Comparison content with structured data (Product schema, ItemList)
- Definitive guides with clear methodology sections
- Original research with specific metrics and named sources
- Content with VideoObject schema linking to related video content

## Video Content as Search Strategy

YouTube is the second-largest search engine. Every content strategy must include a video component for high-value topics.

### YouTube-as-Search Principles
- **Search intent alignment**: Match video titles and descriptions to the exact queries people type into YouTube search, not just Google
- **Keyword research**: Use YouTube autocomplete, TubeBuddy, and vidIQ in addition to traditional SEO keyword tools — YouTube search intent often differs from Google
- **Transcript optimization**: YouTube auto-generates transcripts that are indexed for search — ensure spoken content includes target keywords naturally
- **Chapter timestamps**: Add structured chapters (0:00 format) that match subtopic keywords — these appear in YouTube search results and are used by AI engines
- **VideoObject schema**: Embed VideoObject structured data on the blog page where the video is hosted — this connects the video content to Google SERP features and AI citation

### Video Content Briefs
When creating video content briefs, include:
- Target search queries (YouTube + Google)
- Competitor video analysis (top 5 videos for the query — note retention patterns, hooks, gaps)
- Key differentiator (what does this video offer that the top 5 do not?)
- Blog pillar page to cross-link (for Topic Domination Plan integration)
- Thumbnail concept (readable at mobile size, high contrast, max 3 words)
- First 30 seconds script (the hook — most critical for retention)
